Apple iPhone 14 And iPhone 14 Plus Review: Minor Upgrades For A Worthy Contender

Oct 14, 2022,06:34pm EDT

The Apple iPhone 14 is the least expensive current iPhone you can buy—and that $200 savings over an iPhone 14 Pro is its main appeal. It’s the first iPhone to consider if you don’t want to splash out on an iPhone 14 Pro and don’t need the three cameras or always-on display the extra money buys. The iPhone 14 is a worthwhile contender, even if its external design appears largely the same as last year’s model. Inside, it is an all-new handset. The larger iPhone 14 Plus—the first 6.7-inch iPhone introduced without the corresponding price bump that comes with being a Pro model—shares those new internal guts of the standard iPhone. Now, the biggest iPhone is accessible to many more pockets.

IPhone 14 Pro's Camera

The biggest change to the iPhone 14 Pro’s camera is the addition of a new 48-megapixel quad-pixel main sensor that Apple says enables better photos and new features like an additional 2x telephoto for a total of four zoom options. While the main camera is obviously the main star of the show, Apple says that all the cameras have received a boost in low-light performance thanks to a new Photonic Engine.


Apple iPhone 14: Pricing

iPhone 14 series was launched globally last week. Apple announced four new models this year namely, iPhone 14, iPhone 14 Plus, iPhone 14 Pro, and iPhone 14 Pro Max. This time around the iPhone mini has been removed and the company has added the iPhone Plus model to the portfolio. The iPhone 14 series this time around comes with incremental upgrades over the last-gen iPhone 13 lineup. Interestingly, the iPhone 14 and iPhone 14 Plus are powered by the last-gen A15 Bionic chip while the Pro and Pro Max are the two devices to get the new and the latest A16 Bionic SoC. The RAM capacity has been increased by 2GB on the vanilla model and the battery capacities on the iPhone 14 are also similar to the last gen.
